# DORA: Disruption-Optimized Risk Assurance
## The Idea Document

---

## 1. Introduction

DORA (Disruption-Optimized Risk Assurance) is a next-generation AI-powered parametric micro-insurance platform designed to protect the income of gig delivery workers in real time.

Gig workers face unpredictable income disruptions due to:
- Weather conditions
- Traffic congestion
- Civic restrictions such as strikes and curfews
- Platform-side downtime

Traditional insurance models fail in this context due to:
- Manual claims
- Slow processing
- High premiums
- Low accessibility

DORA solves this by introducing:
- Per-order micro-premium deductions
- Real-time disruption detection
- AI-based income loss estimation
- Instant automated payouts with no claims required

The system is delivered through a lightweight mobile application optimized for accessibility across mid-range devices.

---

## 2. Core Concept: Parametric Micro-Insurance

DORA operates on a parametric insurance model where payouts are triggered based on predefined real-world conditions rather than manual claim submissions.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Event-based triggers
- No paperwork or claim filing
- Instant payout execution
- Transparent and predictable logic

**Comparison:**

| Traditional Insurance | DORA |
| :--- | :--- |
| Claim-based | Trigger-based |
| Manual verification | Automated validation |
| Delayed payouts | Wallet-based payouts |
| Fixed premiums | Dynamic micro-deductions |

## 3. Integrated Insurance Policy Design

### 3.1 Premium Collection Model

DORA uses a micro-deduction system embedded into user earnings.

**Formula:**
```
Premium per Order = (Base Plan % × Dynamic Risk Multiplier) × Order Earnings
```

The dynamic risk multiplier is generated by the AI Risk Scoring engine, which analyzes the worker's historical data, typical delivery zones, time of day, and upcoming weather patterns to adjust the premium fairly. A rider in a monsoon-heavy zone will have a slightly different risk profile than one in a quiet suburb, ensuring actuarial soundness without penalizing the user unfairly.

**Plans:**

| Plan | Base Deduction Rate | Weekly Cap |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Basic | 2% | ₹40 |
| Standard | 4% | ₹60 |
| Pro | 6% | ₹80 |

**Key Features:**
- Automatic deduction after each order
- Weekly cap to ensure affordability
- No upfront payment burden
- AI-powered dynamic weekly pricing based on individual risk scoring

### 3.2 Coverage Model

**Payout Formula:**
```
Payout = Avg Hourly Income × Lost Hours × Coverage %
```

| Plan | Coverage | Max Daily | Weekly Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Basic | 60% | ₹150 | ₹700 |
| Standard | 75% | ₹300 | ₹1000 |
| Pro | 90% | ₹400 | ₹1200 |

### 3.3 Coverage Activation

Coverage becomes active when:
- User enrolls in a plan
- At least one premium deduction occurs in the current week

*This is tentatively planned and may be subject to change.*

### 3.4 Trigger Conditions

Payout is triggered automatically when:
- A verified disruption occurs
- User is within the affected zone
- Activity drops significantly
- Fraud checks are cleared

### 3.5 Exclusions and Fair Usage

Not covered are:
- Voluntary inactivity
- GPS spoofing
- Intentional inactivity
- Non-disruption-related downtime

## 4. End-to-End Mobile Application Workflow

### Persona Deep Dive: Meet Raj

Raj is a 24-year-old delivery partner in Siliguri. He works ten-hour shifts on a Zepto or Blinkit-like platform, earning approximately ₹500 to ₹800 on a good day. He lives in a paying guest accommodation and sends money home to his family in a smaller town every week. He uses a mid-range Android phone such as a Redmi Note series and is very data-conscious, worrying about battery drain and mobile data costs. His biggest fear is a sudden rainstorm or a platform glitch that costs him a day's wage, pushing him behind on his weekly savings goal. He does not have time to file insurance claims or understand complex paperwork. He needs protection that works automatically in the background without him having to think about it.

**Step 1: User Onboarding**

The user registers with:
- Name
- Phone number for OTP verification
- Delivery platform ID such as Zepto or Blinkit ID
- Location
- UPI ID and bank details
- Vehicle and license details
- Government ID proof
- Face verification through face scan

In the backend:
- A unique worker ID is generated
- A user profile is created
- A geo-cluster is assigned

**Step 2: Policy Enrollment**

The user selects from:
- Basic, Standard, or Pro plans

The user interface shows:
- Deduction percentage
- Coverage percentage
- Weekly cap
- Maximum payout

**Step 3: Passive Premium Deduction**

- Premium deduction is calculated automatically per order
- A weekly cap is enforced
- Coverage is activated after the first deduction

**Step 4: Active Monitoring Phase**

The system continuously tracks:
- GPS with optimized polling for battery life
- Weather APIs
- Traffic APIs
- Civic disruptions
- Platform activity
- Crowdsourced regional data
- Cellular tower and network encryptions for fraud detection using data spoofing applications

**Step 5: Disruption Detection**

Triggers include:
- Heavy rainfall
- Natural disasters
- Curfews or protests
- Platform outages

**Step 6: Income Impact Analysis**

The system compares:
- Expected earnings from an ML model trained on historical gig data
- Actual earnings

If a significant drop is detected, the process proceeds.

**Step 7: Fraud and Validation Layer**

Checks include:
- GPS authenticity
- Activity consistency
- Time correlation
- Behavioral anomalies

**Step 8: Payout Calculation**

- Loss is calculated
- Coverage percentage is applied
- Caps are enforced

**Step 9: Instant Payout to Wallet and Weekly Settlement**

- The calculated payout amount is instantly credited to the user's in-app DORA wallet. This process can be triggered multiple times a day as disruption events are detected and validated.
- Users can view their real-time wallet balance in the app.
- Withdrawals from the DORA wallet to the user's linked bank or UPI account are processed on a fixed, pre-defined schedule such as every Monday at 10:00 AM or twice a week based on the user's policy terms. This encourages users to treat the wallet as a protected savings buffer and optimizes the payout reconciliation process.
- The user is notified when a payout is added to their wallet and when the balance is withdrawn to their bank.

**Step 10: Dashboard Update**

The user sees:
- Earnings protected
- Claim history
- Active coverage
- Alerts
- Current wallet balance
- Next withdrawal date

## 5. System Architecture

### 5.1 Frontend Architecture

**Tech Stack:**
- React Native with TypeScript for cross-platform compatibility on iOS and Android, ensuring usability on Raj's device
- Redux Toolkit or Zustand for state management to enable seamless UI updates
- React Native Paper for pre-built UI components for rapid development

**Modules:**
- Authentication
- Policy
- Dashboard
- Notifications
- Payments including wallet view

### 5.2 Backend Architecture

**API Gateway:**
- Entry point
- Authentication
- Rate limiting

**Microservices:**
- **User Service:** Manages profiles, onboarding, and KYC
- **Policy Service:** Handles enrollment, premiums, and coverage logic
- **Monitoring Service:** Polls external APIs and user GPS data
- **Disruption Engine:** Core AI layer that detects events and calculates impact
- **Wallet and Payout Service:** Manages user wallets, credits payouts instantly, and handles scheduled bulk withdrawal processing to bank and UPI accounts

### 5.3 AI and Data Layer

**Models:**
- **Demand Prediction Model** using XGBoost or LightGBM to predict expected deliveries per hour based on historical data, day of week, and weather, establishing the baseline for expected income
- **Anomaly Detection Model** using Isolation Forest or Autoencoder to detect fraud patterns such as GPS spoofing or impossible travel speeds

**Data Pipeline:**
- Apache Kafka to ingest real-time event streams including GPS pings and weather alerts
- Python with FastAPI and Celery for processing and feature engineering for ML models
- PostgreSQL for structured storage of all transactional data

### 5.4 Database Design

**PostgreSQL stores:**
- Users
- Policies
- Transactions
- Events
- Claims
- Wallet balances and transaction logs

**Redis handles:**
- Caching
- Sessions
- Real-time triggers

## 11. AI/ML Capabilities

**Demand Prediction:**
- Inputs: Historical earnings, day of week, time, weather forecast
- Output: Expected income for the next hour as the baseline for measuring disruption impact
- Technology: XGBoost or LightGBM for tabular data

**Disruption Detection:**
- Inputs: Weather alerts, traffic congestion indices, civic event flags
- Output: Verified disruption events
- Technology: Combination of rule-based thresholds such as rainfall exceeding 20 millimeters per hour with clustering to identify regional anomalies

**Fraud Detection:**
- Inputs: GPS coordinates, speed, time at location, app usage patterns
- Output: Fraud score from 0 to 100
- Technology: Isolation Forest or Autoencoder to detect outliers from normal behavior. If Raj's GPS shows him traveling at 200 kilometers per hour in the middle of Siliguri traffic, the model flags it.

**Risk Scoring:**
- Inputs: Aggregated features from all above models and historical payout data
- Output: Dynamic risk multiplier for premium calculation
- Technology: Logistic regression or a simple neural network that updates weekly as new data arrives

## 13. End-to-End System Flow

1. Raj completes deliveries for Zepto or Blinkit in Siliguri.
2. ₹2 is deducted from his earnings representing 2 percent of a ₹100 order and allocated to his DORA premium.
3. A sudden unforecasted torrential downpour hits Raj's delivery zone near Sevoke Road in Siliguri.
4. The DORA Disruption Engine cross-verifies:
   - Weather API data showing rainfall exceeding 30 millimeters per hour in Raj's geo-cluster
   - Other significant unforeseen climatic disruptions or natural disasters in his area
5. The AI Demand Prediction model calculates Raj should have earned ₹250 in the next two hours based on historical data, but his actual earnings are ₹50.
6. The fraud check verifies Raj's GPS was active and consistent with being stuck in the rain, with no spoofing detected.
7. Payout calculation shows a loss of ₹200. With Standard Plan coverage at 75 percent, the payout is ₹150. The daily cap of ₹300 is not exceeded.
8. The Payout Service instantly credits ₹150 to Raj's in-app DORA wallet.
9. Raj receives a notification in Bengali stating that heavy rain was detected in his area and ₹150 has been added to his DORA wallet to cover his lost income, with the next withdrawal to his bank scheduled for Monday.
10. On Monday at 10:00 AM, the system automatically processes the withdrawal, transferring the wallet balance to Raj's linked UPI ID.
11. Raj opens the app and sees his updated earnings protected total and his current wallet balance on the dashboard.
